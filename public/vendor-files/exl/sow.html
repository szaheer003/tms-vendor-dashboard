DRAFT STATEMENT OF WORK

Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) Services

  1. Structure and Governing Documents

This Statement of Work (“SOW”) is issued by Fidelity Information Services, LLC (“FIS”), inclusive of the TSYS business, and will form part of the definitive outsourcing agreement entered into between FIS and the successful bidder (“Service Provider”).

This SOW is governed by:

  • The FIS Master Services Agreement (“MSA”), and
  • This SOW, which shall prevail in the event of conflict for SOW‑specific matters.
  1. Term and Renewal
  • Initial Term: Five (5) years from the applicable Service Commencement Date.
  • Renewal: Up to two (2) one‑year extensions at FIS’s sole discretion, subject to updated SOW terms as agreed between the parties, including pricing for years 6 and 7.
  • FIS retains the right to terminate individual service towers, geographies, or volumes without impacting the remainder of the SOW; provided that such terminations will be considered partial terminations for which termination fees will be applicable if such termination reduces the fees under this SOW by 10% or more during any given year.
  1. Scope of Services

Service Provider shall deliver the BPO services set forth in the responsibility matrix set forth below to support the following FIS processes:

  • Customer Service (Tier 1 & Tier 2)
  • Fraud Operations (basic and complex)
  • Disputes and Chargebacks
  • Back‑office Processing
  • Debt & Collections
  • Ancillary and ad‑hoc operational support

  1. Service Levels and Performance
  • SLAs, KPIs, and credits will be defined in the definitive SOW exhibits.
  • Material Persistent SLA failure of critical service levels constitutes grounds for:
    • Step‑in rights
    • Volume reallocation
    • Partial or full termination for cause

Service Provider commits to continuous performance improvement, including measurable productivity gains over the contract term that will be agreed between the parties and set forth in the definitive SOW.

  1. Pricing Principles (RFP Position)
  • Pricing to be submitted as part of the RFP response, in accordance with the requirements set out in section ‘3.2. Commercial Offer to Include’ of the RFP statement document.
  • All COLA and inflationary impacts must be fully baked into the proposed rates for the seven (7) year committed pricing term. No annual price uplifts shall apply.
  • Benchmarking rights apply annually to ensure top‑quartile market competitiveness.
  1. Commercial Value Commitments

Service Provider is expected to commit to the following value levers:

6.1 Rebates / Gainshare

  • Rebates linked to:
    • Revenue scale
    • Productivity improvements
    • Digital enablement
  • Gainshare mechanisms for efficiency improvements beyond agreed thresholds.

6.2 Productivity Commitments

  • Minimum annual productivity improvements (baseline to be agreed).
  • Failure to achieve minimum commitments may trigger service credits or fee adjustments.
  1. Transformation and Technology
  • Service Provider responsible for funding and delivering transformation initiatives aligned to:
    • Automation
    • Digitisation
    • Quality and productivity uplift
  • Any Service Provider tools deployed on FIS infrastructure:
    • Must be licensed to FIS for internal use
    • Survive termination where embedded in FIS technology or operations
  1. Governance, Audit and Compliance
  • Robust governance model aligned to FIS standards.
  • Audit rights:
    • Annual
    • Regulatory‑driven
    • Incident‑driven
  • Compliance with:
    • PCI‑DSS
    • GDPR / UK DPA
    • FCA / EBA / PRA (where applicable)
  • Mandatory cooperation with FIS client and regulator audits.
  1. Workforce and Rebadging Principles

Where services transition from TSYS/FIS in‑house operations:

  • Service Provider may be required to:
    • Rebadge eligible employees
    • Maintain salary parity at point of transfer
    • Comply with TUPE or equivalent local regulations
  • Service Provider responsible for:
    • Workforce continuity
    • Backfilling
    • Retention management
  1. Termination Rights

FIS may terminate:

  • For cause (material breach, persistent SLA failure, regulatory risk)
  • For convenience, subject to a notice period and declining termination fee schedule
  • Partially, by service tower, geography, or volume
  • Termination assistance services to be provided for up to 24 months, at contracted rates.
  1. Confidentiality and Publicity
  • No public reference to FIS or TSYS without prior written approval.
  • All RFP materials and resulting contracts treated as confidential.
  1. RFP Positioning Note

This SOW represents FIS’s intended commercial framework. Final contractual terms will be confirmed with the selected supplier.

  1. Go To Market Arrangements

Service Provider will partner exclusively with FIS to deliver the contracted services to FIS/TSYS clients. Service Provider may not independently bid on, pursue, or deliver the contracted services to FIS/TSYS clients independently from FIS/TSYS.
