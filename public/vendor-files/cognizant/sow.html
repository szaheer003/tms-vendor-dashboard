DRAFT STATEMENT OF WORK

Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) Services

  1. Structure and Governing Documents

This Statement of Work (“SOW”) is issued by Fidelity Information Services, LLC (“FIS”), inclusive of the TSYS business, and will form part of the definitive outsourcing agreement entered into between FIS and the successful bidder (“Service Provider”).

This SOW is governed by:

  • The Main Consulting Services Agreement effective October 1, 2023 (as amended, the “MSA”), and
  • This SOW, which shall prevail in the event of conflict for SOW‑specific matters.
  1. Term and Renewal
  • Initial Term: Five (5) years from the applicable Service Commencement Date.
  • Renewal: Up to two (2) one‑year extensions at FIS’s sole discretion, subject to the SOW terms (excluding pricing terms)
  • FIS retains the right to terminate individual service towers, geographies, or volumes provided, however, that (i) FIS guarantees a minimum committed volume of [X]% of the total contracted scope during the term of the SOW, and (ii) in the event of any such partial termination, FIS shall pay Cognizant a termination fee equal to [X] months of the fees attributable to the terminated tower, geography, or volume, as compensation for stranded costs and resource reallocation .
  1. Scope of Services

Service Provider shall deliver BPO services including:

  • Customer Service (Tier 1 & Tier 2)
  • Fraud Operations (basic and complex)
  • Disputes and Chargebacks
  • Back‑office Processing
  • Debt & Collections
  • Ancillary and ad‑hoc operational support

Services may be delivered via:

  • Onshore, Nearshore, Offshore locations
  • Dedicated or blended delivery models, including WFH and/or WFO.
  1. Service Levels and Performance
  • Service Provider will support service levels aligned with applicable, reasonable standard requirements.
  • SLAs, KPIs, and credits will be defined and approved by Service Provider and FIS in the definitive SOW exhibits.
  • Persistent SLA failure constitutes grounds for the following remedies, applied in a progressive manner:
    • The implementation of a Corrective Action Plan (CAP). Upon recurring failure to meet the agreed SLAs, the Service Provider shall submit a formal Corrective Action Plan within [X] business days of written notification by FIS. The CAP must identify the root cause(s) of the underperformance, define specific remediation actions, assign accountable owners, and establish measurable milestones with target dates. FIS reserves the right to review, request amendments to, and approve the CAP. The Service Provider shall provide periodic progress updates until full compliance is restored.
    • In the event the CAP does not result in sustained performance recovery, the parties shall escalate the matter to senior management for resolution.

Service Provider commits to continuous performance improvement, including measurable productivity gains over the SOW term.

  1. Pricing Principles
  • All COLA and inflationary impacts must be fully baked into the proposed rates for the seven (7) year committed pricing term. No annual price uplifts shall apply.
